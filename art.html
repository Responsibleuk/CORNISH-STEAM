Creative Collaborations

Cornwall, with its breathtaking natural landscapes and vibrant artistic community, has been a fertile ground for creativity and innovation in the arts and creative industries. Here is a summary of key advances in art and the creative industries that have originated from Cornwall:  

St Ives School of Painting

The St Ives School of Painting, established in the early 20th century, played a pivotal role in the development of modern British art. Artists such as Ben Nicholson, Barbara Hepworth, and Peter Lanyon emerged from this artistic community, pioneering abstract and modernist movements.

Impact: The St Ives School of Painting put Cornwall on the map as a center of artistic excellence. Its influence can still be felt today, with Cornwall continuing to attract artists, collectors, and enthusiasts from around the world.

Cornwall Film Festival

The Cornwall Film Festival, founded in 2002, has become an important platform for showcasing local and international filmmaking talent. The festival celebrates independent films, supports emerging filmmakers, and promotes Cornwall as a filming location.

Impact: The Cornwall Film Festival has nurtured a vibrant film community in the region, fostering creativity, collaboration, and cultural exchange. It has also contributed to the growth of the local film industry, attracting productions and providing opportunities for local talent.

Tate St Ives

The Tate St Ives, an offshoot of the renowned Tate galleries, opened in 1993. It hosts a diverse range of contemporary and modern art exhibitions, showcasing both local and international artists.  

Impact: Tate St Ives has been instrumental in promoting contemporary art and raising the profile of Cornish artists on a global scale. It has provided a platform for artists to exhibit their work, fostering creativity, dialogue, and cultural exchange within the artistic community.  

Outdoor Arts

Cornwall has a vibrant outdoor arts scene, with festivals and events that celebrate street performance, circus arts, and large-scale public installations. Examples include the Falmouth International Sea Shanty Festival and the biennial Man Engine event.  

Impact: Outdoor arts festivals and events in Cornwall have transformed public spaces into dynamic stages, engaging the community and attracting visitors. They contribute to the local economy, inspire creativity, and provide opportunities for artists and performers to showcase their work.
