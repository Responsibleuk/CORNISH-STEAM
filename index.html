About

Cornish Steam

“We stand on the brink of a technological revolution that will fundamentally alter the way we live, work, and relate to one another. In its scale, scope, and complexity, the transformation will be unlike anything humankind has experienced before. We do not yet know just how it will unfold, but one thing is clear: the response to it must be integrated and comprehensive, involving all stakeholders of the global polity, from the public and private sectors to academia and civil society.” - Klaus Schwab. Founder and Executive Chairman, World Economic Forum Geneva

The Fourth Industrial Revolution challenges us to consider the ethical and social implications of new technologies, and to ensure that they are developed in a way that benefits society as a whole. Overall there is a transformative power of technology and the need for a coordinated response to ensure that this transformation benefits all members of society. For art, the Fourth Industrial Revolution presents new opportunities for artists to experiment with technology and explore the intersection of the physical, digital, and biological spheres. Artists can use technology to create new forms of art that are interactive, immersive, and responsive to the environment. This can also lead to new ways of experiencing and understanding art, as well as new ways of engaging with audiences in the STEM fields.
