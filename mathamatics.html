Overview

Mathematics, specifically geometry, is crucial in advanced technologies as it forms the basis for precise measurement, visualization, and modelling. It plays a key role in computer graphics, CAD and 3D modelling, robotics and motion planning, computer vision, 3D printing, and computational physics and engineering simulations. Mathematical concepts such as vectors, transformations, geometric algorithms, and geometric representations are used to create realistic computer-generated imagery, design objects digitally, enable robot navigation, analyse visual data, fabricate 3D-printed objects, and simulate physical phenomena. While Cornwall may not be widely recognized as a hub for mathematical research, prominent mathematicians have links with Cornwall and made significant contributions to the discipline.

William Ernest Frazer (1840-1920):

Born in Penzance, William Ernest Frazer was a prominent mathematician and educator. He made significant contributions to the theory of functions and mathematical analysis. Frazer also co-authored the book "A Treatise on Differential Equations" with his colleague A. R. Forsyth, which became a standard reference in the field.

George Boole (1815-1864):

George Boole spent a significant part of his life in the region. Boole was a mathematician and logician who developed Boolean algebra, which forms the foundation of modern digital logic and computer science. His work on symbolic logic has had a profound impact on mathematics, philosophy, and computer programming.

Thomas Fowler (1777-1843):

Born in Truro, Thomas Fowler was a renowned mathematician and astronomer. He made important contributions to the fields of spherical trigonometry and celestial mechanics. Fowler's work on lunar theory and the motion of planets earned him recognition among his peers.

Arthur Harold Stone (1916-2000):

Arthur Harold Stone spent a significant part of his life in the county. Stone was a distinguished mathematician known for his work in combinatorial mathematics, graph theory, and coding theory. He made significant contributions to the field, including Stone's theorem on graph colouring.

Mary Cartwright (1900-1998):

Mary Cartwright spent a considerable part of her life in Cornwall. She was a highly esteemed mathematician and the first woman to be appointed as a mathematics professor in the University of Oxford. Cartwright made substantial contributions to the study of nonlinear differential equations, chaos theory, and the theory of functions.

In mathematics, as in many other fields, the contributions of women have often been overlooked or undervalued. However, it is crucial to recognize and celebrate the achievements of women mathematicians, by embracing diverse perspectives, experiences, and backgrounds, the mathematical community fosters creativity, innovation, and new ways of approaching problems. Encouraging the participation of individuals from different gender identities, ethnicities, and cultural backgrounds helps to break down barriers and ensure a more inclusive environment.
